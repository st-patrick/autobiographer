# Research plan: How to interview someone about their life

## Core question
What are the best practices, frameworks, and question sets for interviewing
someone (yourself, a parent, etc.) about their life history — especially for
recovering memories from periods that aren't directly accessible?

## Why this matters
The Autobiographer app needs better interview prompts. Currently it uses a
hardcoded pool of ~12 questions per age group. We want:
1. Questions that actually unlock memories (not generic small-talk)
2. Frameworks for interviewing third parties (e.g. user's mom) about user's
   own life
3. Sequencing (what to ask first, what builds rapport)

## Search queries

1. `StoryCorps great questions list interview` — StoryCorps has the best-known
   curated question library; it's a reference standard
2. `oral history interview methodology best practices` — academic / institutional
   methodology
3. `memoir writing prompts childhood memories sensory` — creative writing angle,
   sensory triggers
4. `reminiscence therapy questions framework dementia` — clinical use of memory
   prompts; tested for actually unlocking memory
5. `life story interview McAdams research` — Dan McAdams' "Life Story Interview"
   is a major academic instrument
6. `interview parent about your childhood questions` — practical guides for
   adult children interviewing parents
7. `proust questionnaire personality questions` — classic short instrument

## What I'm looking for

- **Question lists** that are battle-tested
- **Frameworks** for sequencing (chronological? thematic? sensory?)
- **Techniques** for unlocking specific memory types (smells, songs, places)
- **Third-party interview** advice (interviewing someone about a third person)
- Differences in approach by **age range** of the subject
