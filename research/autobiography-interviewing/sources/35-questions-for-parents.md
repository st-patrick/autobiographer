# 35 Life Interview Questions for Parents

The article organizes questions into three life stages: **Childhood, Youth, and Adulthood**.

## Childhood Questions (1-11)

1. What do you think of when you think of growing up in [hometown]?
2. What did you love to do as a child before primary school?
3. What did you love to do in school?
4. What do you remember most about your mother (grandmother)?
5. What was most important to her?
6. What do you remember most about your father (grandfather)?
7. What was most important to him?
8. If Grandma and Grandpa had a message for you and their grandchildren, what do you think it is?
9. What message do you have for your siblings that you want them to always remember?
10. When you think about growing up with siblings, what did your everyday life look like?

## Youth Questions (12-13)

11. What do you remember most about your teenage years?
12. How did you meet my mother and know she was the only one?

## Adulthood Questions (14-35)

13. How do you endure each other for 61 years and still counting?
14. How did you choose your career and what was your favorite part of it?
15. What made you successful at work?
16. What did you believe in yourself that helped you succeed?
17. What times in your life "really tested" you, and what did you learn?
18. Which three events characterize your life the most?
19. What do you remember about when we were all born?
20. Were you ever afraid to be a parent?
21. Name the 3 most important values you'd like to pass on.
22. What three words represented your approach to parenting and why?
23. When you think of my mother, how would you describe her?
24. What message do you have for my mother that you want her to always remember?
25. What three words best describe who you were trying to be in life?
26. What do you want your children to focus on regarding careers?
27. What have you learned about other people in life?
28. What does the world need more of right now?
29. What do you think people want most in life?
30. What were the three best decisions you ever made?
31. What are you most proud of in life?
32. What were five of the most positive moments in your life?
33. What message would you like to share with your family?
34. What are you most grateful for?
35. Are you afraid of death?

## Notes for our use

Q19 ("What do you remember about when we were all born?") and Q20 ("Were you ever afraid to be a parent?") are exactly the kind of questions the user could ask his mom about his own infancy/early childhood (the 1991-1992 gap).

The list is heavily values-oriented (3 best decisions, 3 important values, 3 words). That framing is repetitive but effective — it forces compression and reveals what's actually important.
