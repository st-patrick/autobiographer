# Oral History Best Practices: Key Guidelines

## Preparation

The OHA emphasizes that "First-time interviewers and others involved in oral history projects should seek training" regardless of project scope. Key preparation steps include:

- Selecting an appropriate repository early that aligns with project goals and can enforce agreements
- Obtaining informed consent and documenting the process with proper forms
- Choosing narrators based on "the relevance of their experiences to the subject at hand, while striving to identify and incorporate as many diverse voices as possible"
- Conducting pre-interview discussions to establish rapport and clarify expectations
- Researching "primary and secondary sources, as well as through social engagement with individuals"
- Creating "a high-quality recording of the interview (audio or video format)"
- Developing an open-ended interview guide covering key themes

## The Interview

Interviewers should:

- Conduct sessions "in a quiet location with minimal background noises and possible distractions"
- Record contextual lead-ins with names, date, location, and proposed subject matter
- Ask open-ended questions and follow-ups seeking "additional clarification, elaboration, and reflection"
- "Respect the rights of interviewees to refuse to discuss certain subjects, to restrict access, or choose a pseudonym"
- Balance "the objectives of the project and the perspectives of their narrators"
- Secure signed legal release forms ideally upon interview completion

## Preservation

Critical archival practices include:

- Depositing oral histories "in a repository such as a library or archive that has the capacity to ensure long-term and professionally managed preservation"
- Storing recordings "according to established archival standards designated for the media format used"
- Making "efforts to preserve electronic files in formats that are cross platform and nonproprietary"
- Creating "redundant digital copies" stored in "different physical locations" immediately after completion
- Organizing "related material for each interview—photographs, documents, or other records"

## Access & Use

Repositories should:

- Provide "transcripts, indexes with time tags linking to the recording, detailed descriptions"
- "Honor the stipulations of prior agreements made with the interviewers or sponsoring institutions"
- "Make a good faith effort to obtain" consent forms if missing
- "Take all steps practicable to abide by any restrictions set forth by the narrator"

Users must practice intellectual honesty by:

- Avoiding "stereotypes, misrepresentations, and manipulations of the narrator's words"
- "Retaining the integrity of the narrator's perspective"
- Verifying information presented as factual
- Contextualizing excerpts and "providing a citation to the location of the full oral history"
