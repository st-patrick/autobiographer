# StoryCorps Great Questions (sample, from search results)

## Operating principles

- Most participants get through about **6 questions in a 40-minute interview**
- Recommended: select **6–8 questions** from the list before the interview
- Add a few of your own — "the best questions are the ones you've always wanted to ask"

## Sample questions (from search result summaries)

### Conversation starters
- How would you like to be remembered?
- What has been the happiest moment of your life so far?

### Wisdom transfer
- For generations listening to this years from now, is there any wisdom you'd want to pass on to them?
- What would you want them to know?

### The unsaid
- Is there anything that you've never told me but want to tell me now?

### Self-counsel
- If you could talk to a younger version of yourself, what would you say?

### Relationship reflection
- What were your first impressions of me? How did they change?

### Curiosity
- If you could interview anyone from your life living or dead, but not a celebrity, who would it be and why?

### Influence
- Was there a teacher or teachers who had a particularly strong influence on your life? Tell me about them.

## Notes for our use

The **"6 questions per 40 minutes"** rule is striking — that's slow. Each question is meant to invite a long answer, not a short one. Our current interview mode asks one question per year, which is faster than StoryCorps' pace but probably correct for written entries.

The recurring theme across StoryCorps questions: **time-bridging** — asking about messages to/from past or future versions of self, what you'd say to people not present, etc. This creates the "telling your grandkids" framing the user already uses (line 2 of the original autobiography file: "you are telling your grandchildren about this day, these days").

The most valuable categories the user doesn't yet leverage:
- "Is there anything you've never told me?" (vulnerability)
- "What would you say to younger you?" (counsel)
- "Who would you most want to interview?" (curiosity reveals values)
